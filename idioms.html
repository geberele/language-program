Idioms

Idioms are expressions whose meaning doesn't come from the words themselves. If you translate them literally, you get nonsense — so you just have to know them. These 100 are the ones you'll meet most often in everyday English.

How to use this page

  • Register matters. Most of these are neutral or informal — fine in conversation and friendly writing, usually not in formal reports.
  • Don't try to bend them. Idioms are fixed: it's raining cats and dogs, never it's raining dogs and cats.
  • Start by recognizing them when you hear them. Producing them naturally comes later.
