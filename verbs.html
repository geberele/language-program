Verbs & Tenses

Verb tenses

English has twelve active tenses, made from three times (past, present, future) and four aspects (simple, continuous, perfect, perfect continuous). Learn one column and you've learned a third of the grid.

The 12 tenses at a glance

SimpleContinuousPerfectPerfect continuous
Present I work I am working I have worked I have been working
Past I worked I was working I had worked I had been working
Future I will work I will be working I will have worked I will have been working

Present simple

Form. subject + base verb (+ -s for he/she/it).

Use. Facts, habits, routines, general truths, schedules.

Water boils at 100°C.

She takes the train every morning.

Watch out. Third-person -s is the classic slip: he goes, not he go.

Present continuous

Form. am/is/are + verb-ing.

Use. Actions happening right now, temporary situations, arranged future plans.

I'm reading a book on grammar.

We're meeting Sara tomorrow at 6.

Present perfect

Form. have/has + past participle.

Use. Past actions with present relevance; life experiences; things started in the past that continue.

I have lived in Rome for ten years.

She has never eaten sushi.

Signal words. for, since, ever, never, just, already, yet.

Present perfect continuous

Form. have/has been + verb-ing.

Use. Emphasizes duration of an action continuing up to now.

I've been studying English since 9am.

Past simple

Form. verb + -ed (regular) or irregular form.

Use. Completed actions at a specific past time.

We moved to Milan in 2019.

Past continuous

Form. was/were + verb-ing.

Use. An action in progress at a past moment, often interrupted.

I was cooking when the phone rang.

Past perfect

Form. had + past participle.

Use. An action that finished before another past action.

By the time we arrived, the film had already started.

Past perfect continuous

Form. had been + verb-ing.

Use. Duration of an action continuing up to a past moment.

She had been waiting for an hour when the bus finally came.

Future with "will"

Form. will + base verb.

Use. Predictions, spontaneous decisions, promises.

I think it will rain tomorrow.

Future with "going to"

Form. am/is/are going to + base verb.

Use. Plans already decided; predictions based on present evidence.

Look at those clouds — it's going to rain.

Future continuous

Form. will be + verb-ing.

Use. An action in progress at a future moment.

This time tomorrow I'll be flying to Lisbon.

Future perfect

Form. will have + past participle.

Use. An action that will be completed before a future moment.

By June, I will have finished the course.
