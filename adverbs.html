Grammar

Adverbs

Adverbs modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. They answer the questions how?, where?, when?, how often?, and to what degree?.

Types of adverbs

TypeAnswersExamples
Mannerhow?quickly, carefully, well, badly
Placewhere?here, there, outside, everywhere
Timewhen?now, yesterday, later, soon
Frequencyhow often?always, usually, often, sometimes, never
Degreehow much?very, quite, too, enough, almost
Certaintyhow sure?probably, definitely, certainly, maybe

Forming adverbs from adjectives

  • Most adjectives: add -ly. quick → quickly.
  • Ends in -y: change y → i, add -ly. happy → happily.
  • Ends in -le: drop the e, add -y. simple → simply.
  • Ends in -ic: add -ally. basic → basically.
Irregulars. good → well, fast → fast, hard → hard, late → late. Hardly and lately exist but mean different things (scarcely; recently).

Position in the sentence

Adverbs of manner

Usually after the verb or after the object.

She sings beautifully.

He opened the box carefully.

Adverbs of frequency

Before the main verb, but after be.

I always drink coffee in the morning.

She is never late.

Adverbs of time and place

Usually at the end of the sentence. Place comes before time.

We met at the café yesterday.

Adverbs of degree

Before the adjective, adverb, or verb they modify.

It's very cold today.

She drives quite fast.
